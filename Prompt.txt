I want to build a Python-based web scraping agent that monitors the 
California Public Utilities Commission (CPUC) website for newly published 
voting meeting documents and sends me email alerts via Yahoo Mail when 
they appear. The agent will be deployed on PythonAnywhere (free tier) so 
it runs 24/7 without needing my PC to be on.

GOAL:
Monitor two CPUC document pages per meeting cycle — the Current Meeting 
Agenda and the Hold List — and send separate Yahoo Mail email alerts when 
each is published.

MONITORING TARGETS:

1. CURRENT MEETING AGENDA
   URL: https://docs.cpuc.ca.gov/SearchRes.aspx?DocTypeID=1
         &DocTitleStart=Current%20Meeting%20Agenda&Latest=1
   - Detect when a new meeting date appears in the document title
   - Also verify by matching the expected agenda number
   - Expected ~10 days before meeting date

2. HOLD LIST
   URL: https://docs.cpuc.ca.gov/SearchRes.aspx?DocTypeID=1
         &DocTitleStart=Hold%20List&Latest=1
   - Detect when a new Hold List appears for the current meeting date
   - Only check AFTER agenda has been confirmed published
   - Expected sometime within the 10 days before the meeting

HOW THE PAGES WORK:
- Both URLs stay constant — they are search queries that always 
  return the latest matching document
- Each returns one result with the meeting date and agenda number 
  in the document title
  e.g. "Current Meeting Agenda for July 16, 2026 (Agenda #3584)"
- Each includes a direct PDF link and a published date
- A change is detected when the meeting date or agenda number 
  in the title matches the next expected meeting

ALERT EMAILS:
Send a separate Yahoo Mail email for each document:

Email 1 — Agenda Alert:
  Subject: "CPUC Voting Meeting Agenda Published — July 16, 2026 
            (Agenda #3584)"
  Body: Meeting date, agenda number, PDF link, 
        date/time detected (Pacific time)

Email 2 — Hold List Alert:
  Subject: "CPUC Hold List Published — July 16, 2026 (Agenda #3584)"
  Body: Meeting date, agenda number, PDF link, 
        date/time detected (Pacific time)

SMART CHECKING SCHEDULE:
Use upcoming meeting dates in config.json to drive all logic.
Two phases per meeting cycle:

PHASE 1 — AGENDA MONITORING:
- More than 12 days before meeting: do not check anything
- 12 days before meeting: check agenda once per day
- 10-11 days before meeting: check agenda every 3 hours
- 8-9 days before meeting: check agenda every hour (safety net)
- Once agenda detected and alert sent: mark confirmed in 
  last_seen.json and begin Phase 2

PHASE 2 — HOLD LIST MONITORING:
- Begins automatically once agenda is confirmed published
- Check Hold List every 3 hours
- Increase to every hour within 3 days of meeting date
- Once Hold List detected and alert sent: mark confirmed 
  in last_seen.json
- Stop all checking once meeting date has passed
- Look to next meeting in config.json and reset cycle

GENERAL RULES:
- Never check Hold List before agenda is confirmed published
- Never check anything more than 12 days before a meeting
- All timestamps in Pacific time
- Log every check with timestamp and result

CREDENTIALS AND CONFIG:
